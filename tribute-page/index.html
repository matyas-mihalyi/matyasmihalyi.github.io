Jimi Hendrix

Jimi Hendrix was one of the most influental guitarists of all time, although, sadly, his career spanned just a few years. You can find out some interesting facts about him on this tribute page.

Early Years

Family

Jimi in his mother's arms as a baby

Jimi was born in 1942, to Lucille and Al Hendrix. His mother was 16 when she got pregnant with Jimi. Al was an alcoholic and often abused his wife and sons. Lucille died in 1958. Jimi got his first guitar in the same year, and began practicing tirelessly.

Army

Jimi got his first electric guitar, in 1959. He played a few gigs before he had to join the army in 1961. His guitar followed him shortly, and eventually became a major distraction. So much so, that Hendrix was discharged from the Army in 1963. While in the army, Jimi met Billy Cox, a bass player whom he played with from then on.

Chitlin' Circuit

Jimi and Billy formed the King Kasuals and began to play smaller gigs. In the next three years Jimi played for numerous R&B bands like the Isley Brothers or Little Richard's band. He also appeared on a number of recordings, most notably on Don Covay's "Mercy, mercy". Getting tired from R&B, he tried to explore other possibilites and formed his own band in New York, Jimmy James and the Blue Flames.

Breakthrough

Moving to England

By 1966, Jimi was struggling to make a living. Fortunately, he was noticed by Keith Richard's then-girlfriend, and shortly after that, Chas Chandles, ex-Animals bassist and soon-to-be manager brought him over to England. He got Jimi a bassist, Noel Redding, and a drummer, Mitch Mitchell. With them, the Jimi Hendrix Experience was formed.

Are You Experienced?

The Experience's first single release was Hey Joe and Stone Free, the latter being Jimi's first original tune with lyrics. It was soon followed by Purple Haze and The Wind Cries Mary. In 1967, the trio had enough material to publish their first LP, Are You Experienced? It remained a top-seller in the UK until their second album came out.

Monterey Pop

While he was immensely popular in England, in the US, Jimi was relatively unknown. It all changed when the Experience performed at the Monterey Pop Festival. It was a legendary performance, where Jimi burnt his guitar at the end of their set. You probably have seen the iconic photo somewhere.

End of the Experience

Axis: Bold as Love

The Experience released their second LP in 1967, titled Axis: Bold as Love. The production was even more faster-paced, but quality was much better. Jimi's solo in the final track, Bold as Love was revolutionary. The album most popular song was Little Wing which was inspired by Jimi's mother. Axis reached the top ten in both the UK and the US.
